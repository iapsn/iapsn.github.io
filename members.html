Mission Statement

The mission of IAPSN is to provide local pet sitting professionals with a support network and share educational opportunities or tools. IAPSN strives to educate pet owners about responsible pet ownership and the benefits of pet sitting services. IAPSN supports local shelters and rescue groups in the efforts with caring for and finding responsible homes for pets.

Code of Ethics

Honesty and Integrity

IAPSN members deal with clients and other pet sitters with honesty and integrity and avoid any conduct that could put clients or their animals, residence, or property at risk. Anyone who is convicted of a crime against persons or property, or listed as a registered sex offender, will have his or her IAPSN membership terminated.

Appropriate Channels for Criticism

Members conduct themselves as professionals, and should not engage in public criticism of fellow pet sitters or of IAPSN. Members are expected and required to treat each other with respect and dignity and use of profanity on the forum is strictly prohibited.

Use of IAPSN Name

The IAPSN name may be displayed only by an IAPSN member in good standing.

Pet Sitting is Our Focus

IAPSN recognizes that many pet sitting businesses are rapidly growing and diversifying, and welcomes members who offer complimentary services such as boarding, grooming or training. However, IAPSN’s mission and identity require that membership be limited to those whose primary business is pet sitting.

Sanctions

Failure to comply with this Code of Conduct and Ethics, if substantiated in the opinion of IAPSN, will be grounds for immediate revocation of membership.

Forum Etiquette

You must attend an IAPSN meeting and become a member before gaining access to our Forum. The first time that you gain access, introduce yourself! Let us know you are online. Tell us your name, business name, service area, a short description about your company.

General

  • Do not give anyone outside of our group access to the Forum. Information is confidential.

  • Try to be brief yet complete when sending messages on the Forum.

  • When posting, if you are starting a new topic, create a new “subject line”.

  • Ask yourself: Will this post or response benefit everyone or should I take this as a private conversation offline, direct to the other pet sitter’s email?

  • All messages should be appropriate and in good taste. Members are expected and required to treat each other with respect and dignity and use of profanity on the forum is strictly prohibited.

  • Remember, never post clients’ names, addresses or personal info. Protect your clients!

  • Emails and attachments will be saved for six (6) months maximum.

  • You will lose access to the Forum if you do not submit an IAPSN Membership Application, Proof of Insurance, and $24 annual membership dues (pro-rated your first year) and by February 1st of following years.

Job Postings

When posting available jobs:

  • Do not post clients name, exact address or contact info (even if they give permission). This protects us from certain liabilities. Ask interested members to reply offline direct to your email, not on the Forum. Include your business email in the posting.

  • You may prefer instead to direct the potential client to www.iapsn.com to use the “FIND A PET SITTER” search feature.

When you are interested in a potential job posted on the Forum:

  • Only current members may respond to a job posting.

  • Answer directly to the email of the member who is posting the job. Do not post your reply on the forum for everyone to see. (This is one way we can reduce competitiveness among members.) And, there is no need for everyone else to read your reply.

  • Do not call the pet sitter who posted the job, unless invited to.

  • The most effective response you can send would be an email written as if directly to the potential client stating your name, business name, contact info, especially your website, and maybe a little bit of info about yourself. Your message can then be forwarded directly “as is” to the potential client. This client may receive several interested replies, and she/he can browse through the information at her/his convenience.

  • Realize that it is not first-come, first-serve, but it is about helping the potential customer find their best fit.
